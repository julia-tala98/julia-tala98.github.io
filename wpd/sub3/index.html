Disease and Pests

Bees across the world have been plagued by what scientists have vaguelly called Colony Collapse Disorder (CCD). Numerous studies have been carried out by scientists everywhere and yet, findings are still alarmingly inconclusive about what specifically is causing these pollinators to rapidly die off. In 2013 it was estimated that already approximately 10 million beehives had died due to CCD.

The leading causes for CCD that most scientists are pointing to is the ever increasing concotion of pesticides, fungicides, and insecticides that are being used on crops, as well as varroa mites which drain the blood of both adult bees as well as developing broods, causing deformation.
